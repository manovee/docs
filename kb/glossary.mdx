# Glossary and data model guide

This glossary explains common Tamlr terms in end-user language, with light backend references for support and admin clarity.

## AI Visibility Score

The percent of tested AI responses that mention the selected company.

Backend reference: calculated from `ai_interactions` response metadata.

## AI Interaction

A stored response from an AI model for a specific prompt.

Backend reference: `ai_interactions`.

## Citation

A source URL found in an AI response.

Backend reference: citations are usually stored in response metadata and may also relate to source analysis tables.

## Company

A workspace that owns prompts, products, competitors, analytics, team members, and settings.

Backend reference: `companies`.

## Competitor

A brand tracked against the selected company.

Backend reference: `competitors` and `competitor_mentions`.

## Gap Analysis

A view that helps find sources where AI models cite or discuss a market but do not clearly mention your company.

Backend reference: derived from AI response citations, company website, competitor websites, and mention metadata.

## Google AI Mode

A search-style AI answer source powered through the configured Google AI Mode workflow. In the current backend, this uses SERP API based infrastructure rather than the direct Google model provider.

Backend reference: `ai-search-google-ai-mode` and `serpapi` logs.

## Prompt

A question Tamlr sends to AI models to test visibility.

Backend reference: `prompts`.

## Prompt Category or Topic

A grouping for related prompts.

Backend reference: `prompt_categories`.

## Product

A product, service, or offering associated with the company.

Backend reference: `products`.

## Search Ranking

Google ranking data for keywords connected to a prompt.

Backend reference: `search_rankings` and the `serp-ranking` Edge Function.

## Sentiment

The tone of an AI response toward the company or competitor.

Backend reference: `analyze-sentiment` stores sentiment scores and supporting metadata.

## Source Domain

A domain cited by AI responses, such as your own website, a competitor site, an editorial site, or a community site.

Backend reference: extracted from response metadata on `ai_interactions`.

## Team Role

A role that controls what a member can do inside a company workspace.

Backend reference: company membership records and role RPCs.

## Platform Admin or Superadmin

A user with access to the Admin console for users, plans, AI configuration, provider models, usage analytics, leads, and early access requests.

Backend reference: `user_roles`, `has_role`, and admin-only RPCs.
